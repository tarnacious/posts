I added the ability to sign into this site with Mozilla Persona to comment. Not that I'm expecting people to use it, I've only had a handful of genuine comments since I started the site some years ago. I was just interested in trying it out, which is why I have this site.

It was pretty staight forward as I already only support the concept of signing in with 3rd party authentication providers such at OpenID, Google and Twitter. So adding Persona was just adding another provider, so I didn't need to change how the authentication system works. Here is the commit.

The only thing that is different from the other providers is that I had to use Javascript. It's not that I don't like Javascript, many of posts here have running Javascript in the content. It's just over the several re-writes of this site in different languages I've included less Javascript in the site itself. There was none on this site, I liked it that way.

Another thing I liked is OpenID. I understand it had usability and adoption issues. With OpenID though, I could choose who manages my identity or I could manage it myself. Apparently my email provider (in my case, me) can implement the Persona (BrowswerID) protocol and not depend on login.persona.org to identify me. Perhaps that is somthing I will look into after posting this.

Finally a note on adoption; I was having dinner with Ozlem last night and tried to explain Persona, but was unable to convince her to even try it to sign into this site. To be fair it's only a sample size of one and I think she was hoping dinner conversation wasn't going to be about online identity management.
